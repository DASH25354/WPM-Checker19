Three-minute Training

【19】Rokkō Festival is Kobe University’s school festival held in early November each year. The committee says 17,000 visitors came over two days last year; before COVID, there were times with 36,000 visitors. This year, it will be on November 8 (Sat) and 9 (Sun). Tanabata Festival is a large summer event in early July run by a student council group. Highlights include lighting the stained glass in the main hall and fireworks. At both events, a stage is set up in front of the main building for club and circle performances, including a yukata show. The current stage scaffolding is old, so they plan to buy new stage equipment to improve safety and impact. A crowdfunding drive started in late September and runs until November 15. The target is 1,000,000 yen, and support starts from 1,000 yen. As of October 10, 49 supporters have given 248,779 yen. All funds will go to buying the stage. If the target is met but other funding does not reach the total, the money will be used for stage rental. Supporters will receive a thank-you email and returns like original stickers and notebooks with event characters.

Quiz

The festivals were canceled due to safety concerns. Both festivals draw many visitors and feature stage events, but old equipment needs replacement. A crowdfunding campaign aims to fund new stage equipment, with details on goals and returns. Only the Tanabata Festival will be held this year.
